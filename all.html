STRANDS public datasets

Here are datasets that we used for long-term autonomy.

Witham Wharf RGB-D dataset

The Witham Wharf was collected for purposes of testing RGB-D localization in changing environments. The dataset consists of one training set and three testing sets that cover changes of eight locations in an open-plan office for one year.

Why and how was the dataset gathered

The intended use was to create a dataset to benchmark visual localization in changing environments. See and hear the 2 minute video below. If you are interrested only in how the dataset was collected, simply skip the first minute of the video or click here.



Organisation

Color images of the dataset are arranged into eight folders according to the location they were captured at. Images were captured on a regular basis every 10 minutes and their filename corresponds to the time they were captured at. For example 00000.bmp was captured at midnight the first day, 00012.bmp at 2:10 am and 00145.bmp at 0:10am of the second day of the data collection. Please note that sometimes the robot got stuck and failed to capture an image at a given location. For our method, we needed to substitute these missing images by taking the last previously captured image.

Lincoln lecturer long and deep (dataset)

This dataset contains depth images captured by a stationary Asus Xtion sensor located in a small lecturer office in the University of Lincoln. The sensor captures a depth image every 5 seconds since April 2013 and files are uploaded automagically every day. The data is recorded simply as a sequence of 320x240x16b images, so you need to download a few utilities to convert them to ROS-compatible or Asus-native formats.
